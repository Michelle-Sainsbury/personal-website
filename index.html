About Me

Throughout my career, I have led programs serving individuals, families, veterans, and communities experiencing homelessness, housing instability, behavioral health challenges, and other complex barriers.

I am frequently called upon when programs require stabilization, stronger systems, clearer direction, or a leader who can bring people together around a shared goal.

Leadership Philosophy

Leadership Is Both Strategic and Human

Strong leadership creates clarity during uncertainty, builds systems that support people, and moves organizations beyond immediate problems toward sustainable results.

I believe leaders must be willing to listen carefully, make difficult decisions, communicate expectations clearly, and remain accountable for both outcomes and organizational culture.

My approach is prevention-driven. I look beyond the immediate issue to identify patterns, operational gaps, and underlying conditions that may prevent an organization from achieving its goals.

Where Leadership Meets Innovation

Applying Emerging Technology With Purpose

After decades of leadership in housing and human services, I chose to study artificial intelligence because nonprofit organizations cannot afford to be excluded from the technologies shaping the future.

I am particularly interested in how AI can help organizations improve decision-making, reduce administrative burden, identify service gaps, strengthen program performance, and make important information more accessible.

I do not believe AI should replace tenant advocates, social-service professionals, or experienced human judgment. Technology should support people, improve systems, and expand an organization’s capacity to serve its community responsibly.
