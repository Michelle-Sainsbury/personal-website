My Leadership Story

Three moments that shaped how I lead.

My leadership wasn't built in a boardroom. It was built in the field, through loss, and through the choice to keep learning.

Origin

Choosing Human Services

My leadership begins with purpose and a commitment to people. I chose a career built on showing up for individuals and families navigating some of the hardest chapters of their lives.

Leadership lesson: purpose-driven leaders build trust before they build strategy — and trust is what makes strategy work.
Resilience

Navigating Profound Personal Loss

Losing someone important to me deepened my resilience and my ability to lead others through complexity and change. It taught me how to hold steadiness for a team even when the ground is uncertain.

Leadership lesson: leaders who have carried real loss bring a grounded calm to organizational crisis — teams feel that steadiness and lead better because of it.
Evolution

Returning to Learn Technology at Pursuit

Effective leaders evolve. Returning to the classroom to build technical skills — AI, Git, GitHub, command line — was a deliberate choice to keep growing as a leader, not a departure from leadership.

Leadership lesson: organizations need leaders who can bridge decades of people-leadership with the tools shaping the next decade of work.
